About

Originally from Sri Lanka, I moved to Finland to pursue my passion for robotics, human-computer interaction, and the future of intelligent systems. I am currently a doctoral researcher working at the intersection of AI, multimodal interaction, and human-robot collaboration, with a focus on building more natural, reliable, and human-centric ways for people and robots to work together.

My work spans the development of conversational AI interfaces for collaborative robots, the study of human behaviour during industrial assembly tasks, and the design of safe, trustworthy frameworks that bring large language models into real-world robotics. Through this research, I aim to bridge the gap between people and intelligent machines—empowering robots to understand, respond, and collaborate more like human teammates.

Outside the lab, I enjoy travelling, exploring new cultures, and taking time to recharge through fitness and outdoor activities. I’m driven by curiosity, creativity, and the belief that technology should make human life better, safer, and more meaningful. Always open to new ideas and collaborations, I’m excited about the possibilities that lie ahead—both in research and in life.

Research Interests

My research focuses on trustworthy multimodal AI for human–robot collaboration, using transformer-based models for context understanding, cognitive validation, ambiguity-aware interaction, and robot task planning in smart manufacturing.

Core areas: Multimodal Human-Robot Interaction, Human-Robot Collaboration, Transformer-based AI, Trustworthy AI, Cognitive Robotics, Robot Task Planning, and Smart Manufacturing.
